There is a large number of different types of plots for visualizing data. Of those, however, only a few are frequently used. The most important plots are the following:

  • The histogram consists of bars that indicate the frequency of measurements
  • The scatter plot shows the value of individual measurements as points
  • The bar plot shows measurements as bars
  • The box plot indicates variation by showing the most frequently observed measurements (e.g.Â the median)
  • The Q-Q plot: compare whether two samples have similar distributions
